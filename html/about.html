About Me

I'm Darragh, a 19 year old software developer studying Immersive Software Engineering at the University of Limerick.

I have always been intruiged by computers and the underlying concepts that make them work. When I was about 13 or 14, I learned touch typing, then took it upon myself to learn how to program.

I started with Python, and quickly familiarised myself with core concepts, conventions, and paradigms. I conducted my own research by reading books and articles and by watching YouTube videos, then tested my knowledge by creating whatever mini-projects I could think of.

When I was in my penultimate year of secondary school, I took Computer Science as one of my subjects. There, I learned a wide variety of computer science theory, and was able to refresh my knowledge on Python.

We went on to learn about web development, creating projects using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I took an interest in everything I learned, often spending my free time refreshing or expanding my knowledge, learning about NodeJS, Sass, and various Python libraries I found interesting. I quickly realised how vast the world of computing is, further captivating my inquisitive nature.

My learning spree continues to this day, and I have become an all-round efficient programmer, having acquired back-end and front-end knowledge and experience. As it stands, I am more interested in back-end development, particularly low-level systems programming. My preferred languages are Python, C++, C, and Rust.

So far in college I have studied a wide range of topics, including but not limited to:

  • SQL & Databases
  • Discrete Mathematics
  • Machine Learning
  • Java
  • Python (again)
  • Git & GitHub
  • Data Structures & Algorithms
  • Computer Organisation
  • Software Lifecycles
  • Computer Science Foundations
  • Software Testing
